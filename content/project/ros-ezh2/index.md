## The problem
Canonical EZH2 inhibitors often lack durable response. GBM heterogeneity can hide subtype-specific dependencies.

## What this work shows
- ROS-linked programs stratify GBM into therapeutically distinct subclasses  
- EZH2 exhibits functional dichotomy across ROS states  
- Noncanonical signaling creates a druggable dependency in ROS(+) tumors

## Why it’s useful for collaboration
A framework for linking subtype → mechanism → actionable intervention, and designing stratified studies.
